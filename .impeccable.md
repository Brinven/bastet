# Bastet — Design Context

> One-time design context for all design work in this project (impeccable/shape).
> The single governing product constraint: **dead simple** — a non-technical volunteer
> makes an adoptable-animal flyer in under 3 minutes on first use.

## Design Context

### Users
Volunteer coordinators and foster families at small animal rescues. **Non-technical**,
time-poor (the person making the flyer also cleans kennels and drives transport), often on
**mobile or an aging laptop**. Frequently rushed and a little stressed — "a dog needs a home
tonight." They have likely never opened Canva, or tried and bounced off its complexity.
No account required for the primary flow (Tier 1, anonymous).

The job to be done: turn one phone photo + a few facts about an animal into a share-ready
adoption flyer, fast, without feeling like they need design skills.

### Brand Personality
**Bright · hopeful · spirited** — with a quiet thread of warmth and care. Bastet (Egyptian
cat goddess of protection) by Axly's Customs. The tool should feel like an encouraging friend
who makes you look good: upbeat and joyful ("let's find them a home!"), never clinical,
never intimidating. Trustworthy and calm underneath the cheer — these users are stressed and
the tool must feel *safe* and *forgiving*. Open-source and privacy-respecting (no telemetry);
that honesty is part of the personality.

Identity expression: **subtle nod**. A small cat-glyph + "Bastet" wordmark and occasional
warm-gold details. Tasteful and professional — not costume-y Egyptian theming.

### Aesthetic Direction
Bright, warm, and friendly with real character — *not* the generic rounded-Poppins
"friendly SaaS" look, and *not* AI-slop (no cyan-on-dark, no purple gradients, no glass).
The editor chrome stays calm and uncluttered so the **animal's photo and the flyer are the
stars**; energy comes from warm color, generous rounding, and lively (not bouncy) motion.

**Theme:** match device (`prefers-color-scheme`), defaulting to light. Both light and dark
must be first-class. Light = warm paper/cream; dark = warm charcoal-brown (never cold gray,
never pure black/white).

**Type system** (UI chrome only — distinct from the curated *flyer* fonts in `src/lib/fonts.js`):
- **Display / wordmark / headings:** `Gabarito` — warm, expressive, a little spirited.
- **UI / body / labels:** `Hanken Grotesk` — friendly humanist grotesque, highly legible at
  small sizes for the field panel.
- Deliberately avoids the banned/reflex set (Inter, DM Sans, Poppins-as-UI, Fraunces, Syne).
- Modular scale, ≥1.25 ratio, fewer sizes with more contrast.

**Color** (define exact tokens in OKLCH at build time):
- Brand hue ≈ amber/marigold **gold** (the Bastet "gold detail") as the primary accent.
- A sparing warm **coral/persimmon** secondary for joyful pops (CTAs, selected states).
- Warm cream/sand neutrals (light) and warm charcoal-brown (dark), all tinted toward the
  gold hue (chroma ~0.005–0.02) for cohesion. 60-30-10 by visual weight; accents stay rare.
- No pure #000/#fff. No gray text on colored fills — use a tint of the fill.

### Design Principles
1. **Three-minute rule beats everything.** If a touch adds polish but costs comprehension or
   speed for a first-time non-technical user, cut it. Progressive disclosure: the obvious path
   first, advanced options tucked behind clear reveals.
2. **The photo and flyer are the hero; the chrome is the supporting cast.** UI recedes; the
   canvas gets the emphasis, the light, and the space.
3. **Warm and forgiving, never clinical or punishing.** Encouraging microcopy, soft warnings
   (e.g. low-res photo) that never block, undo-friendly interactions, big tap targets.
4. **Mobile-first and touch-real.** Designed for thumbs on a phone first, not shrunk from
   desktop. Drag/zoom the photo to reframe — never auto-crop (cutting ears/tails breaks trust).
5. **Distinctive, not templated.** Character through warm color, type pairing, and one or two
   memorable details (the cat-glyph, gold hairlines) — not through AI-slop tells.

### Accessibility (baseline, applied without asking)
WCAG AA contrast in both themes; visible focus rings; ≥44px touch targets; honor
`prefers-reduced-motion`; the curated flyer fonts must be fully loaded before export so the
PNG never substitutes a system font.
